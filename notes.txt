TMMC Programming Assignment - Notes on problems tackled
=======================================================

1. JPEG is lossy, so there is no "pure black/white".
   MS Paint draws in pure black (0,0,0) on pure white (255,255,255), but after
   JPEG compression the sharp line edges become bands of grey, and even "flat"
   areas drift a few values away from 0/255. Testing a pixel for exact black
   would be unreliable.
   -> Solved by classifying a pixel as black using a perceived-brightness
      threshold rather than an exact colour match.

2. Choosing which pixels to inspect.   
   The spec only guarantees presence in both halves + continuity, which simply
   means the line crosses the boundary between the halves: the MIDDLE row.
   -> Solved by scanning the single middle row (Height/2). It is the one row
      every valid line is guaranteed to cross, needs no tunable parameters,
      and is the most robust single choice.


Possible future improvements (not required here):
- GetPixel is convenient but slow; LockBits would be faster for large images.
